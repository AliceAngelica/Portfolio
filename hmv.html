Usability Testing

Typeform User Research Questions

The next phase of the project was to conduct some user testing using the Typeform website to ask the user 5 participants several questions. The questions focused on various aspects of hmv including the design and functionality of the website, and I also wanted to find out if individuals still buy cds and dvds. Below is a list of all of the questions included in the questionnaire:

  1. Please find Sony wireless headphones and add them to your basket. How was your experience finding the product and adding it to the basket?
  2. When was the last time you purchased a CD?
  3. When was the last time you brought a DVD?
  4. How would you describe the website design in three words?
  5. How well do you think the content is laid out?
  6. Rate how easy the website was to navigate?
  7. Do you own a DVD or CD player (or a laptop that can play CDs)?
  8. Would you say the navigation bar and side bars are well laid out?
  9. What do you feel could be improved?


Usability Test Report: HMV Website


Analysis

The usability test aimed to evaluate the functionality and user experience (UX) of the HMV website, which specializes in selling CDs, DVDs, vinyl, merchandise, and more. Five participants were engaged in observing the website and responding to a series of questions.


Q1, Finding Products and Adding to Basket

All participants successfully found Sony wireless headphones and added them to their basket. This demonstrated that the website's functionality for product search and purchase was efficient and satisfactory.


Q2 & 3. Relevance of CDs and DVDs:
  • CD Purchases: 80% of participants reported having purchased a CD over a year ago.
  • DVD Purchases: Similarly, 80% of participants stated their last DVD purchase was over a year ago, with 20% indicating they had never bought a DVD. These findings raise questions about the ongoing relevance and profitability of selling CDs and DVDs.

Q4. Website Design:
    Participants described the website design in various ways:
  • Positive Attributes: "Offerings are clear," "Good visuals - colorful and lively," "Engaging, interactive."
  • Negative Attributes: "Too colorful, too 'boxed,' too overloaded," "Cluttered, difficult to navigate and overwhelming."
    • The majority of participants criticized the cluttered nature of the homepage, suggesting a need for improvement in visual organization.

Q5. Content Layout:

Opinions on the content layout were divided among participants, indicating a lack of consensus on this aspect of the website.


Q6. Navigation Ease:

Most participants rated the website's navigation as 5 or above on a scale, suggesting it was generally easy to navigate.


Q7. Device Ownership:

60% of participants reported owning a device capable of playing CDs or DVDs, highlighting a significant portion of the target audience.


Q8. Evaluation of Navigation Bars:

75% of participants believed that the navigation bars on the website were well laid out, indicating a positive perception of this feature.


Q9. Areas for Improvement:
    Participants suggested several areas for improvement, including:
  • Layout and Menu: Improvement in layout, menu bars, and navigation.
  • Cross-Selling and Social Proof:** Incorporation of cross-selling suggestions and social proof elements.
  • Banner Size: Reduction in the size of the banner for better visual balance.
  • Scrollable Dropdown Bars:** Ensuring dropdown bars are scrollable to prevent items from being cut off.
  • Category Presentation:** Revamping the category presentation, possibly by using images or sub-categories.

The majority of responses highlighted user interface (UI) concerns, emphasizing the need for enhancements in layout, navigation, and visual organization.


Conclusion:

The usability test identified both strengths and areas for improvement in the HMV website. While the functionality for product search and purchase was efficient, there were concerns regarding the cluttered homepage and the relevance of selling CDs and DVDs. Implementing the suggested improvements could enhance the overall user experience and potentially increase user engagement and satisfaction with the website.
